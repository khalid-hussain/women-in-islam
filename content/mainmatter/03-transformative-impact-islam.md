
## **The Transformative Impact of Islam**

Some time ago, in the well-known Egyptian journal *al-Manār*, an article was
published under the title, *Women in Islam* ([المرأة في الإسلام]{dir="rtl"}). In
it, a brief history of women's rights was recorded, and from its study, it
becomes evident that in ancient times, in nearly every region and among almost
every nation of Europe and Asia, women were regarded as a degraded and inferior
creation. The practice of female infanticide found among certain Arab tribes was
also a result of this notion, namely that the birth of a daughter was considered
a cause of disgrace and shame among their peers. In short, there was scarcely
any part of the earth where the rights of this delicate creation were not
trampled upon with cruelty.

The life-giving water of Islam infused a new spirit into this lifeless body, and
you may gauge its extent from the statement of ʿUmar [رضي الله عنه]{dir="rtl"}:

"We never gave significance to women in the days of Jāhilīyah, but when Islam
came, and Allah mentioned their rights, we came to realize that they have rights
over us." \[Ṣaḥīḥ al-Bukhārī\]

This statement is, in reality, a mirror reflecting the attitudes that prevailed
regarding women in pre-Islamic Arabia, and it also demonstrates that Islam
brought about a significant enhancement in women's rights, indeed opening an
entirely new chapter in this regard.
