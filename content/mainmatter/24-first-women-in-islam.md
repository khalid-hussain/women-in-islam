
## **The First Women in Islam**

Under this heading, we intend to highlight those matters in which women attained
precedence and distinction. Among these early distinctions are such merits that,
even if nothing else were recorded in the register of women's deeds, they alone
would suffice to tilt the scale of their prominence in the history of Islam.

The first response to the call of Islam from the Messenger of Allah
[ﷺ]{dir="rtl"} came from a woman. It is a unanimously agreed matter that
Khadījah bint Khuwaylid [رضي الله عنها]{dir="rtl"} was the very first to embrace
Islam. \[Sīrat Ibn Hishām\]

The first person to attain the rank of martyrdom in Islam was a woman. Her name
was Sumayyah bint Khayyāṭ [رضي الله عنها]{dir="rtl"}, the mother of ʿAmmār ibn
Yāsir [رضي الله عنه]{dir="rtl"}. She was martyred by Abū Jahl. It is stated in
*Usd al-Ghābah*, "She was the first martyr in Islam."

When the Quraysh plotted to assassinate the Prophet [ﷺ]{dir="rtl"}, it was a
woman who first conveyed the warning.[^12]

Among Muslims, the use of a bier (tabūt) was first introduced by Asmāʾ bint
ʿUmays [رضي الله عنها]{dir="rtl"}. She was among those who migrated to
Abyssinia, where this practice was common among the Christians, and she
introduced it to the Muslims. From this, it is also inferred that if a
commendable practice exists among other people, it is permissible to adopt it.
\[Ṭabaqāt Ibn Saʿd\]

During the Hijrah, the first person to host the Prophet [ﷺ]{dir="rtl"} was a
woman named Umm Maʿbad [رضي الله عنها]{dir="rtl"}. \[Sīrat Ibn Hishām\]

The desire to participate in naval Jihād was first expressed by a woman. The
Prophet [ﷺ]{dir="rtl"} was resting in the house of Umm Ḥarām bint Milḥān [رضي
الله عنها]{dir="rtl"}. Upon awakening from sleep, he [ﷺ]{dir="rtl"} smiled. She
asked the reason, and he [ﷺ]{dir="rtl"} said, "I saw some people from my Ummah
sailing upon the sea, engaged in Jihād."

She requested, "Pray that I may be among them." He [ﷺ]{dir="rtl"} replied: "You
are among them." Accordingly, during the caliphate of ʿUthmān ibn ʿAffān [رضي
الله عنه]{dir="rtl"}, when the expedition to Cyprus was launched, she
participated in it and attained martyrdom after falling from her mount. \[Ṣaḥīḥ
al-Bukhārī\]

The first hospital in Islam was established during the Prophetic era under the
supervision of a woman named Rufaydah al-Aslamiyyah [رضي الله عنها]{dir="rtl"}.
It was set up within Masjid al-Nabawī itself. \[Sunan Abī Dāwūd\]

The first to affirm the miracles of the Prophet [ﷺ]{dir="rtl"} was a
woman---again, Khadījah bint Khuwaylid [رضي الله عنها]{dir="rtl"}. \[Ṣaḥīḥ
al-Bukhārī\]

The earliest practical rejection of gold (in the sense of treating it as
insignificant for worldly attachment) was initiated by a woman. It is mentioned
in *Al-Kāmil fī al-Tārīkh* that ʿAlī ibn Abī Ṭālib [رضي الله عنه]{dir="rtl"}
first stayed in Madīnah at the house of a widow. He observed that a man would
come daily and leave something behind. Upon inquiry, it was said, "Sahl ibn
Ḥunāyf [رضي الله عنه]{dir="rtl"} breaks the idols of his people and gives them
to me, and I use them as fuel."[^13]
