
## **The Messenger of Allah [ﷺ]{dir="rtl"} and Women**

Above, we have clarified through certain verses of the Noble Quran and the
statements of the Master of Creation [ﷺ]{dir="rtl"} the status accorded to women
in our religion, and the powerful and effective guidance that has been given for
their care and for harmonious conduct. We now wish, with brevity, to demonstrate
how the Prophet [ﷺ]{dir="rtl"} himself dealt with the women in practical terms,
for his actions and conduct are for us like red sulphur[^4] (*al-kibrīt
al-aḥmar*) in the journey of life.

Allah says,

[لَقَدْ كَانَ لَكُمْ فِي رَسُولِ اللَّهِ أُسْوَةٌ حَسَنَةٌ]{dir="rtl"}

*Indeed, in the Messenger of Allah you have an excellent example.* \[Sūrat
al-Aḥzābʾ, Q33:21\]

The esteem and honor which the women held in the blessed heart of the Prophet
[ﷺ]{dir="rtl"} may be gauged from the narration of Anas [رضي الله
عنه]{dir="rtl"} where the Messenger of Allah [ﷺ]{dir="rtl"} said, "In this
world, women and perfume have been made dear to me, and my comfort has been
provided in prayer." \[Sunan al-Nasāʾī\]

The nature of the Prophet's [ﷺ]{dir="rtl"} conduct with his pure wives [رضي الله
عنهن]{dir="rtl"} can be understood by examining their biographies, though
detailing them here would lead to unnecessary length. However, his noble
character was not confined to them alone; rather, other women also benefited
from its sweet effects.

On the occasions of the two ʿĪds, which are the two grand assemblies of the
Muslims, as well as on other occasions, the Prophet [ﷺ]{dir="rtl"} would give
special consideration to women in his sermons. Thus, Ibn ʿAbbās [رضي الله
عنهما]{dir="rtl"} relates that once the Prophet [ﷺ]{dir="rtl"} delivered an
address in a large gathering, and the rows of women were at a distance. He
perceived that perhaps they had not fully received the instructions and
exhortations, so he himself went to them, taught them the necessary matters, and
instructed them to give charity. This occurred on the occasion of ʿĪd al-Fiṭr,
and Bilāl [رضي الله عنه]{dir="rtl"} was with him. A garment was spread, and the
women, deeply moved, began removing their jewelry and placing it into it.
\[Ṣaḥīḥ al-Bukhārī\]

At certain times, women in that era demonstrated similar enthusiasm. This is, in
reality, a Sunnah, and one should avoid neglecting it as much as possible.
Today, in the country, there exists an educational conference in which a
separate section has been designated for women. We have heard many elders, who
are not fully acquainted with the true practice of the Lawgiver [ﷺ]{dir="rtl"},
say that in this corrupt age, the status of women has been elevated to such an
extent that separate assemblies are established for them. Yet they are unaware
that the foundation of this practice was laid in that blessed era, following
whose footsteps is an obligation upon all of us.

On another occasion, a delegation of women came to the court of Prophethood and
complained, "The men have prevailed over us in attending your gatherings, so
appoint for us a day from your time." The Prophet [ﷺ]{dir="rtl"} listened
attentively to their request and then designated a specific day for them, from
which they benefited through his instruction. \[Ṣaḥīḥ al-Bukhārī\]

In our view, it is a religious duty upon Muslim women that, in order to fulfill
this Sunnah, they organize a separate gathering wherein scholars of religion and
guides of the community inform them of the present needs of the country as well
as the essential rulings and etiquettes of the religion.

On one occasion, Umm Hānī bint Abī Ṭālib [رضي الله عنها]{dir="rtl"} granted
protection to a man. ʿAlī [رضي الله عنه]{dir="rtl"} considered this grant
invalid and intended to fight him. Umm Hānī complained to the Prophet
[ﷺ]{dir="rtl"}, who prevented ʿAlī [رضي الله عنه]{dir="rtl"} and said to Umm
Hānī, "O Umm Hānī, we grant protection to whom you have granted protection."
\[Ṣaḥīḥ al-Bukhārī\]

It appears that ʿAlī [رضي الله عنه]{dir="rtl"} initially thought that the
granting of protection by a woman would not be valid, but the Prophet
[ﷺ]{dir="rtl"} declared this act to be valid and binding, thereby teaching that
women too have the right to participate in such matters.
