
## The Selflessness of Muslim Women

Just as women stood alongside men in all the noble qualities of the early
generations, they were not behind in the quality of self-sacrifice (*īthār*).
This trait was among the distinguishing features of that era. The Quran
highlights,

\AYAH_AR_START
وَيُؤۡثِرُونَ عَلَىٰٓ أَنفُسِهِمۡ وَلَوۡ كَانَ بِهِمۡ خَصَاصَةٞۚ
\AYAH_AR_END

\AYAH_START
They prefer others over themselves, even though they themselves are in need.
(Sūrat al-Ḥashr, Q59:9)
\AYAH_END

In this path, the gentler gender traversed such difficult stages that even among
men, examples of similar magnitude are rarely found. Women are known for their
deep love for their children, yet for the sake of the call of Islam and their
true religion, they sacrificed even their beloved offspring.

Umm Sulaym \hon{\P_HER}{\BODY}, whose incident you have already read, was
extremely poor. When the Messenger of Allah \hon{\PBUH}{\BODY} migrated to
Madīnah, every person presented whatever gift they could, for that day was one
of immense joy and celebration for them. The greatest and most blessed favor had
set foot in their land, and the sun of Prophethood had risen in the valleys of
Madinah. On that blessed day, the people of Madīnah came out in great numbers to
welcome him, and young girls joyfully recited:

Umm Sulaym \hon{\P_HER}{\BODY} also wished to present a gift but was constrained
by poverty. Finally, she brought her young son, Anas \hon{\P_HIM}{\BODY}, and
presented him to the Prophet \hon{\PBUH}{\BODY}, saying, “I offer him to you.
Keep him in your service, and supplicate for him for long life and abundance of
wealth.” The Messenger \hon{\PBUH}{\BODY} accepted this gift with pleasure and
supplicated for Anas \hon{\P_HIM}{\BODY}.

This is the same Anas \hon{\P_HIM}{\BODY} who served the Prophet
\hon{\PBUH}{\BODY} for ten years, lived to the age of ninety-one, and later
governed Baṣrah during the caliphate of ʿUmar \hon{\P_HIM}{\BODY}. What greater
example of sacrifice for religion than that a poor woman offered the very piece
of her heart and beloved child in dedication to Islam?

Among the campaigns of the Messenger of Allah \hon{\PBUH}{\BODY}, the Day of
Uḥud holds a distinct renown. This battle was, in many respects, a great trial
for the Muslims. The renowned leader of the Muslims and one of the most valiant
warriors of Arabia, Ḥamzah \hon{\P_HIM}{\BODY}, attained martyrdom in this very
battle. He was killed by Wahshī, the slave of Hind bint ʿUtbah, who struck him
with a spear. It was the same Wahshī who later killed Musaylimah the Liar in the
Battle of Yamāmah. His famous statement is well-documented: “At Uḥud I killed
the best of people, and at Yamāmah I killed the worst of people.”

Hind bint ʿUtbah mutilated the body of Sayyid al-Shuhadāʾ (Ḥamzah
\hon{\P_HIM}{\BODY}); she took out his liver, chewed it, and severed various
parts of his body. The scene was so heart-rending and grief-stricken that any
human being would naturally lose composure. For this reason, the Messenger
\hon{\PBUH}{\BODY} instructed that his aunt Ṣafiyyah \hon{\P_HER}{\BODY} should
not be allowed to see the body, lest she be unable to bear it.

However, when Ṣafiyyah \hon{\P_HER}{\BODY} learned of this and came to
see her brother's body, her son Zubayr \hon{\P_HIM}{\BODY} (the disciple
of the Messenger \hon{\PBUH}{\BODY}) conveyed the Prophet's instruction to her. Yet
she replied, “I am aware that my brother Ḥamzah \hon{\P_HIM}{\BODY} has
been mutilated. Why do you stop me? To be sacrificed in the path of Allah in
such a manner is no extraordinary matter.” \[Ṭabaqāt Ibn Saʿd\]

Women of that age might have filled the heavens with cries of lamentation, but
this lion-hearted woman showed not even a crease upon her brow. Such is the
affair of Islam, that any hardship or suffering in the path of Allah is borne
with serenity and composure:

Whatever comes from the Beloved is good.

In one battle, all the relatives of an Anṣārī woman were martyred. When people
came to inform her, her first question was, “Is the Messenger \hon{\PBUH}{\BODY}
safe?” When she was given the glad tidings of his safety, she was overwhelmed
with joy and said, “If the Noble Prophet \hon{\PBUH}{\BODY} is safe, then
thousands of lives may be sacrificed for him.” \[Sīrat Ibn Hishām\]

Similarly, when the Messenger \hon{\PBUH}{\BODY} returned safely to Madīnah
after the Battle of Uḥud, despite having sustained multiple wounds, Kabshah bint
Rāfiʿ, the mother of Saʿd ibn Muʿādh \hon{\P_HIM}{\BODY}, came running and fell
at his feet, saying, “In comparison to your safety and well-being, everything
else is insignificant.”

Umm Sulaym \hon{\P_HER}{\BODY} forgave her *mahr* merely because her husband
accepted Islam. \[Sunan al-Nasāʾī\]

Among these accounts, the incident of Asmāʾ bint Abī Bakr \hon{\P_HER}{\BODY} is
especially remarkable. When the Messenger \hon{\PBUH}{\BODY} intended to migrate
from Makkah, provisions were prepared at the house of Abū Bakr
\hon{\P_HIM}{\BODY}. In haste, nothing was available to tie the food. Asmāʾ
\hon{\P_HER}{\BODY} immediately tore her waistband into two pieces, using one to
tie the provisions and the other to tie herself. From that day, she became known
as Dhāt al-Niṭāqayn (the woman of the two waistbands). She was a woman of great
courage and strength. ʿAbdullāh ibn al-Zubayr \hon{\P_TWO}{\BODY} was born to
her. \[Sīrat Ibn Hishām\]

Greater still is the immense sacrifice demonstrated by the Mothers of the
Believers (Ummahāt al-Muʾminīn). When some of the wives of the Prophet
\hon{\PBUH}{\BODY} made certain requests, the following *āyah* was revealed:

\AYAH_AR_START
يَٰٓأَيُّهَا ٱلنَّبِيُّ قُل لِّأَزۡوَٰجِكَ إِن كُنتُنَّ تُرِدۡنَ ٱلۡحَيَوٰةَ ٱلدُّنۡيَا وَزِينَتَهَا فَتَعَالَيۡنَ أُمَتِّعۡكُنَّ وَأُسَرِّحۡكُنَّ سَرَاحٗا جَمِيلٗا ٢٨ وَإِن كُنتُنَّ تُرِدۡنَ ٱللَّهَ وَرَسُولَهُۥ وَٱلدَّارَ ٱلۡأٓخِرَةَ فَإِنَّ ٱللَّهَ أَعَدَّ لِلۡمُحۡسِنَٰتِ مِنكُنَّ أَجۡرًا عَظِيمٗا ٢٩
\AYAH_AR_END

\AYAH_START
O Prophet! Say to your wives, “If you desire the life of this world and its
adornment, then come, I will give you a (suitable) compensation (for divorce)
and let you go graciously. But if you desire Allah, His Messenger, and the
Hereafter, then indeed Allah has prepared for the righteous among you a great
reward.” (Sūrat al-Aḥzāb, Q33:28--29)
\AYAH_END

This was an extremely severe test. When this *āyah* was revealed, the Prophet
\hon{\PBUH}{\BODY} first went to ʿĀʾishah \hon{\P_HER}{\BODY} and said, “I wish
to say something to you, but do not hasten in your reply; consult your parents
first.” After explaining the matter, ʿĀʾishah \hon{\P_HER}{\BODY} replied, “In
such matters, there is no need to consult my parents. I choose Allah and His
Messenger.” \[Sunan Ibn Mājah\]

Thereafter, the Prophet \hon{\PBUH}{\BODY} went to the other wives and informed
them of ʿĀʾishah's response, and all of them affirmed the same choice.

After this profound example, can it not be said that women have left behind
unparalleled and unmatched examples of sacrifice in the history of Islam?

Even ordinary women upheld this spirit. Once, a woman named Saʿīrah al-Asadiyyah
\hon{\P_HER}{\BODY} came to the Prophet \hon{\PBUH}{\BODY} complaining of
illness and requested a supplication for a cure. The Prophet \hon{\PBUH}{\BODY}
said, “If you wish, I will supplicate for you, and you will be cured; but if you
exercise patience, Allah will grant you a great reward.” She immediately
replied, “I choose the reward of Allah.” \[Ṣaḥīḥ al-Bukhārī\]

One of the paternal aunts of the Prophet \hon{\PBUH}{\BODY}, ʿArwá bint ʿAbd\ al-Muṭṭalib
\hon{\P_HER}{\BODY}, had a son named Ṭayyib. When he accepted Islam, Abū Jahl
and other leaders of Quraysh tied him to a pillar and beat him. People came to
his mother and said, “Had he not supported Muḥammad \hon{\PBUH}{\BODY}, this
would not have happened.” Yet she paid no heed to such complaints and would
proudly say, “The best day of his life is the day he supports his cousin.”

Reflect upon all these narrations and consider: is it not correct to say that
women not only stood alongside men in this virtue, but in some cases even
surpassed them? Women of the present age should reflect deeply upon these
incidents. Just as their predecessors demonstrated such sacrifice in service of
religion and religious honor, they too must dedicate their intellectual and
physical capacities with zeal and enthusiasm toward the progress of their
community and the rescue of its declining condition. By spending generously from
their wealth in collective causes, they should demonstrate that the same
religious fervor and communal spirit still flows within them.
