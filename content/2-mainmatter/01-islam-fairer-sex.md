
# Islam and the Fairer Sex

It is said that the religion of Islam has not formulated any specific law
regarding the refinement and cultivation of the subtle gender (*al-jins
al-laṭīf*), nor has it granted them sufficient opportunity to benefit from the
divine blessings in this world. Is this a fact? Certainly not. Indeed, a Muslim
woman is not, according to the beliefs of the Romans, a household possession
over which the husband has the right of sale and purchase, preservation and
destruction; nor is she, according to the religious law of the Greeks, a
powerful devil such that every possible effort should be made toward her
humiliation and degradation. Rather, she is a partner and equal participant
alongside men in sustaining the order of the world. Upon her rests the important
responsibility of nurturing and cultivating the new generation, rectifying and
reforming morals, and strengthening adherence to religion; accordingly, we shall
present clear statements regarding this as we proceed.
