
## The Emphasis on Mothers

There are certain rights in which men and women are equal, yet the Prophet
\hon{\PBUH}{\BODY} gave greater emphasis to the rights of women. For example,
serving one's parents is obligatory upon children, yet the Prophet
\hon{\PBUH}{\BODY} repeatedly gave preference to the mother. In a narration of
*Ṣaḥīḥ al-Bukhārī*, a man asked the Messenger of Allah \hon{\PBUH}{\BODY}, “Who
is most deserving of my good companionship?”

He \hon{\PBUH}{\BODY} replied, “Your mother.” He then asked, “Who next?”

“Your mother,” the Prophet \hon{\PBUH}{\BODY} replied again. He asked, “Who
next?” He \hon{\PBUH}{\BODY} again said, “Your mother.” The man asked again,
“Then who?” Thereupon, he \hon{\PBUH}{\BODY} said, “Then your father.”
\[Ṣaḥīḥ al-Bukhārī\]

To emphasize this right, he \hon{\PBUH}{\BODY} even restrained people from other
virtuous acts. It is reported in *Sunan al-Nasāʾī* that a man named Jāhimah
al-Sulamī came seeking permission to participate in battle. The Prophet
\hon{\PBUH}{\BODY} asked if his mother was alive; upon receiving an affirmative
response, he said, “Remain with her, for Paradise lies beneath her feet.”

The reason for such emphasis is that people, considering women weak and frail,
may become negligent in fulfilling their rights. In any case, the Sharīʿah
safeguarded the rights of women and granted them full authority to assert their
legitimate claims with clarity and strength.
