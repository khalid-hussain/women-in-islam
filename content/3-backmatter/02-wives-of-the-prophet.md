
# Ahl al-Bayt: Wives of the Prophet \hon{\PBUH}{\HEADER} {.unnumbered}

An easy way to memorize their names is through the phrase:

[خَسٌّ مَعَ جَزَرٍ صَحَّة]{dir="rtl"}ٌ

Lettuce with carrots is healthy.

## 1. Khadījah bint Khuwaylid ([خديجة بنت خويلد]{dir="rtl"})

Her lineage met that of the Prophet's \hon{\PBUH}{\BODY} at Quṣayy ibn Kilāb. She
belonged to the Banū Asad sub-tribe of Quraysh. Her father, Khuwaylid, was a
close associate of ʿAbd al-Muṭṭalib and stood alongside him during the incident
of Abraha's attack on Makkah. Her mother was Fāṭimah bint Zāʾidah from Banū
ʿĀmir. She was famously known as al-Ṭāhirah.

Khadījah had previously been married to ʿAtīq ibn ʿĀʾidh al-Makhzūmī, with whom
she had a daughter named Hind. After his death, she married Abū Hālah Hind ibn
Zurārah al-Tamīmī, with whom she had children: Hind, Hālah, Ḥārith, and Ṭāhir.

Her friend Nafīsah approached the Prophet \hon{\PBUH}{\BODY} with her proposal, and
thereafter, Ḥamzah ibn ʿAbd al-Muṭṭalib approached her uncle ʿAmr ibn Asad on
behalf of the Prophet \hon{\PBUH}{\BODY}. She was approximately 28 years old at the
time of marriage, though some scholars state she was 40. This took place 25
years before the Hijrah.

The Prophet \hon{\PBUH}{\BODY} married her at the age of 25, giving a *mahr* of
either 20 young camels or, as reported in other narrations, 12.5 *ūqiyyah*
(approximately 500 dirhams) of silver. The marriage is reported to have taken
place about three months after his return from Shām, toward the end of the month
of Ṣafar. A cow was slaughtered by her family, and Abū Ṭālib delivered a sermon.
The Prophet \hon{\PBUH}{\BODY} also slaughtered camels to feed the guests.

She was the mother of all the children of the Prophet \hon{\PBUH}{\BODY} except
Ibrāhīm. The Prophet \hon{\PBUH}{\BODY} said, "Indeed, Allah did not grant me better
than her; she accepted me when people rejected me, she believed in me when
people doubted me, she shared her wealth with me when people deprived me, and
Allah granted me children only through her." \[Ṣaḥīḥ Muslim\]

She was the first to hear the revelation from the Prophet \hon{\PBUH}{\BODY}, the
first to perform *wuḍūʾ* and pray with him, and the first to believe in his
message. The Prophet \hon{\PBUH}{\BODY} said, "Her love was nurtured in my heart by
Allah Himself." \[Ṣaḥīḥ Muslim\]

He did not marry any other woman during her lifetime. She was the first person
to be given the glad tidings of Jannah, and she was given specific glad tidings
of a palace in Jannah. The Messenger of Allah \hon{\PBUH}{\BODY} conveyed to her the
Salām from Allah and from Jibrīl \hon{\S_HIM}{\BODY}.

She passed away in Makkah in Ramaḍān, three years before the Hijrah, at the age
of either 53 or 65. She was buried in al-Ḥajūn by the Prophet \hon{\PBUH}{\BODY}.
Ṣalāt al-Janāzah had not yet been prescribed at that time.

The Prophet \hon{\PBUH}{\BODY} said, "The best woman of her time was Maryam bint
ʿImrān, and the best woman of this Ummah is Khadījah." \[Ṣaḥīḥ al-Bukhārī\]

## 2. Sawdah bint Zamʿah ([سودة بنت زمعة]{dir="rtl"})

Her lineage met that of the Prophet's \hon{\PBUH}{\BODY} at Luʾayy ibn Ghālib. She
was born in Makkah and was the daughter of Zamʿah ibn Qays from Banū ʿĀmir. Her
mother was al-Shamūs bint Qays from Banū al-Najjār.

She was previously married to al-Sakrān ibn ʿAmr, with whom she had a son, ʿAbd
al-Raḥmān. Together, they migrated twice to Abyssinia. Her mother was also a
cousin of ʿAbd al-Muṭṭalib.

While in Abyssinia, she saw two dreams which were interpreted as indicating her
future marriage to the Prophet \hon{\PBUH}{\BODY}. She later married the Prophet
\hon{\PBUH}{\BODY} in Ramaḍān, in the tenth year of his Prophethood, with a *mahr*
of 400 dirhams. She was approximately 50--55 years old at the time. For three
years, she remained his only wife after the passing of Khadījah \hon{\P_HER}{\BODY}.

The *āyāt* pertaining to Ḥijāb in *Sūrat al-Nūr* were revealed in connection
with her. After the passing of the Prophet \hon{\PBUH}{\BODY}, she chose to remain
within her home and did not go out.

She passed away in Madīnah in Shawwāl 23 AH, at the age of 72, during the
caliphate of ʿUmar ibn al-Khaṭṭāb. She narrated five *aḥādīth* from the Prophet
\hon{\PBUH}{\BODY}.

## 3. ʿĀʾishah bint Abī Bakr ([عائشة بنت أبي بكر]{dir="rtl"})

Her lineage met that of the Prophet's \hon{\PBUH}{\BODY} at Luʾayy ibn Ghālib. She
was the daughter of Abū Bakr al-Ṣiddīq and belonged to the Banū Taym sub-tribe
of Quraysh. Her mother was Umm Rummān Zaynab bint ʿĀmir from Banū Kinānah.

She was born in Makkah approximately four to five years after the beginning of
Prophethood. She was the only virgin whom the Prophet \hon{\PBUH}{\BODY} married.
She was shown to the Prophet \hon{\PBUH}{\BODY} in a dream by Jibrīl \hon{\S_HIM}{\BODY} [عليه
السلام]{dir="rtl"} as his future wife.

She married the Prophet \hon{\PBUH}{\BODY} in Shawwāl in the tenth year of
Prophethood, with a *mahr* of 400 dirhams, at the age of six. The marriage was
consummated when she was nine, and she moved into his household in Shawwāl 1 AH.
She was approximately five to eight years younger than Fāṭimah, and she was born
into Islam.

At the time of the Prophet's \hon{\PBUH}{\BODY} marriage to ʿĀʾishah \hon{\P_HER}{\BODY}, even the pagan Makkans who were seeking to slander him did not
raise objections to this marriage, while they did raise objections to the
marriage with Zaynab. In reality, ʿĀʾishah was already engaged to Muṭʿim ibn
ʿAdī before this. That was the norm of the time, and women attained puberty
faster because of the climate. For over a 1000 years, the Muslim Ummah has had
no issues with the age of ʿĀʾishah.

ʿĀʾishah \hon{\P_HER}{\BODY} said, "If a girl (*jāriyah*) were to reach
puberty at the age of nine, then she is a woman." \[Al-Tirmidhī\]

Imam Ibn Ḥazm \hon{\M_HIM}{\BODY} said, "The age of ʿĀʾishah is recorded in
the texts without a difference of opinion." \[Ḥajjat al-Widāʿ\]

Imam Ibn Kathīr \hon{\M_HIM}{\BODY} said, "This is a matter in which there is
no difference of opinion amongst people." \[Al-Sīrah al-Nabawiyyah\]

Imam Ibn ʿAbd al-Barr \hon{\M_HIM}{\BODY} said, "I do not know of anyone
differing on this." \[Al-Istīʿāb\]

She was the only wife whose parents migrated to Madīnah. Revelation would
descend upon the Prophet \hon{\PBUH}{\BODY} while he was in her company. The rulings
of *tayammum* were revealed in 4 AH in connection with her.

She was around twelve years old at the time of the Incident of Ifk, which
occurred after the expedition of al-Muraysīʿ, before the Battle of al-Aḥzāb and
the events of Banū Qurayẓah in 5 AH.

The Prophet \hon{\PBUH}{\BODY} affectionately called her "Ḥumayrāʾ" and would
shorten her name. She reported, "Once the Messenger of Allah \hon{\PBUH}{\BODY} said
to me, 'O ʿĀʾish! This is Jibrīl greeting you.'"

*Āyāt* of the Quran were revealed, declaring her innocence---to be recited until
the Day of Judgment. She also witnessed Jibrīl \hon{\S_HIM}{\BODY} in a
non-angelic form.

The Prophet \hon{\PBUH}{\BODY} passed away while leaning against her chest when she
was eighteen years old. She became one of the greatest scholars of the Ummah.
Imam al-Ḥākim stated, "One-fourth of the rulings of the Sharīʿah were narrated
on the authority of ʿĀʾishah."

She passed away in Madīnah on 17 Ramaḍān 58 AH at the age of 66. Her *janāzah*
was led by Abū Hurayrah. She narrated 2,210 *aḥādīth* from the Prophet
\hon{\PBUH}{\BODY}.

The Prophet \hon{\PBUH}{\BODY} said, "Many men attained perfection, but among women
only Āsiyah, the wife of Firʿawn, and Maryam bint ʿImrān attained perfection.
And the superiority of ʿĀʾishah over other women is like the superiority of
*tharīd* over all other foods." \[Ṣaḥīḥ al-Bukhārī\]

## 4. Ḥafṣah bint ʿUmar ([حفصة بنت عمر]{dir="rtl"})

Her lineage met that of the Prophet's \hon{\PBUH}{\BODY} at Kaʿb ibn Luʾayy. She was
the daughter of ʿUmar ibn al-Khaṭṭāb and belonged to the Banū ʿAdī sub-tribe of
Quraysh. Her mother was Zaynab bint Maẓʿūn from Banū Jumah.

She was born in Makkah approximately five years before Prophethood, around
eighteen years before the Hijrah, and was close in age to Fāṭimah.

She was previously married at the age of 14 to Khuṇays ibn Ḥudhāfah, who was one
of the first twenty people to accept Islam. She migrated to Madinah along with
her father and husband. Khunais was the only participant of his tribe, the Banu
Sahm, at Badr, and was martyred after sustaining a wound at Uhud.

It is reported that she accepted Islam before her father due to her marriage to
Khuṇays. She later married the Prophet \hon{\PBUH}{\BODY} in Shaʿbān 3 AH with a
*mahr* of 400 dirhams at the age of nineteen.

Her worship was praised by the Angel Jibrīl \hon{\S_HIM}{\BODY}, who said
to the Prophet \hon{\PBUH}{\BODY}, "She is one who frequently fasts and prays, and
she will be your wife in Paradise."

She was the custodian of the first compiled *muṣḥaf* of the Quran during the
caliphate of Abū Bakr. She passed away in Madīnah in 45 AH at approximately
sixty years of age. Her janāzah prayer was led by Marwān ibn al-Ḥakam, the
governor of Madīnah. She narrated 60 *aḥādīth* from the Prophet \hon{\PBUH}{\BODY}.

## 5. Zaynab bint Khuzaymah ([زينب بنت خزيمة]{dir="rtl"})

Her lineage met that of the Prophet's \hon{\PBUH}{\BODY} at ʿAbd Manāf. She belonged
to the tribe of Banū Hilāl. Her father was Khuzaymah ibn al-Ḥārith, and her
mother was Hind bint ʿAwf.

She was born in Makkah approximately thirteen years before the Hijrah. She was
previously married to Ṭufayl ibn al-Ḥārith ibn ʿAbd al-Muṭṭalib, and later to
his brother ʿUbaydah ibn al-Ḥārith. He was the first to lead an expedition under
Islam, and he was martyred from a wound he got at Badr. ʿUbaydah was one of the
first martyrs in Islam. Reports that she later married ʿAbdullāh ibn Jaḥsh are
considered weak.

She married the Prophet \hon{\PBUH}{\BODY} in Ramaḍān 3 AH with a *mahr* of 400
dirhams at around 28--29 years of age. There were approximately twenty days
between her marriage and that of Ḥafṣah. She was the first non-Qurashī wife of
the Prophet \hon{\PBUH}{\BODY}.

She was known as Umm al-Masākīn due to her compassion and generosity toward the
poor. She was also the maternal (uterine) sister of Maymūnah bint al-Ḥārith.

She passed away in Madīnah in Rabīʿ al-Awwal 4 AH at the age of 30. The Prophet
\hon{\PBUH}{\BODY} himself led her *janāzah* prayer. She was the only wife for whom
he performed *janāzah* and the last of his wives to pass away during his
lifetime.

She remained married to the Prophet \hon{\PBUH}{\BODY} for only two to three months,
though some reports extend this to eight months, placing her passing in Rabīʿ
al-Ākhir 4 AH.

## 6. Umm Salamah Hind bint Abī Umayyah ([هند بنت أبي أُميّة]{dir="rtl"})

Her lineage met that of the Prophet's \hon{\PBUH}{\BODY} at Murrah ibn Kaʿb. She was
born in Makkah and was the daughter of Abū Umayyah ibn al-Mughīrah from Banū
Makhzūm. Her mother was ʿĀtikah bint ʿĀmir from Banū Kinānah.

She was the foster sister of ʿAmmār ibn Yāsir. She was previously married to Abū
Salamah ʿAbdullāh ibn ʿAbd al-Asad, with whom she had children: Salamah, Durrah,
ʿUmar, and Zaynab. Salamah was the first child born to the Muslims in Abyssinia.

She and her husband were among the earliest to migrate---twice to Abyssinia and
later to Madīnah. Abū Salamah, who was also the foster brother of the Prophet
\hon{\PBUH}{\BODY}, passed away from wounds sustained at Uḥud.

Umm Salamah reported, "I heard the Messenger of Allah \hon{\PBUH}{\BODY} saying,
'When a person suffers from a calamity and utters, "We belong to Allah and to
Him we shall return. O Allah! Compensate me in my affliction, recompense my loss
and give me something better in exchange for it," then Allah surely compensates
him with reward and a better substitute.'" Umm Salamah said, "When Abū Salamah
died, I repeated the same supplication as the Messenger of Allah \hon{\PBUH}{\BODY}
had commanded me with. So Allah bestowed upon me a better substitute than him."
\[Ṣaḥīḥ Muslim\]

She married the Prophet \hon{\PBUH}{\BODY} in Shawwāl 4 AH at the age of 29. After
marriage, she moved into the residence of Zaynab bint Khuzaymah. Her daughter,
originally named Barrah, was renamed Zaynab by the Prophet \hon{\PBUH}{\BODY}.

Several verses of the Quran were revealed in response to her questions. She also
witnessed the Angel Jibrīl \hon{\S_HIM}{\BODY} [عليه السلام]{dir="rtl"} in a non-angelic form. She
was the only Mother of the Believers alive at the time of the martyrdom of
Ḥusayn at Karbalāʾ.

She passed away in Madīnah in Shawwāl 62 AH at approximately 84 years of age.
Her *janāzah* was led by Abū Hurayrah. She was the last of the Prophet's
\hon{\PBUH}{\BODY} wives to pass away. She narrated 378 *aḥādīth*, making her the
second-highest narrator among the Mothers of the Believers after ʿĀʾishah.

## 7. Zaynab bint Jaḥsh ([زينب بنت جحش]{dir="rtl"})

Her lineage met that of the Prophet's \hon{\PBUH}{\BODY} at Khuzaymah ibn Mudrikah.
She was born in Makkah and was the daughter of Jaḥsh ibn Riʾāb from Banū Asad.
Her mother was Umaymah bint ʿAbd al-Muṭṭalib from Banū Hāshim, making her the
cousin of the Prophet \hon{\PBUH}{\BODY} and the closest to him in lineage among his
wives.

She was born seventeen years before Prophethood and was originally named Barrah,
a name later changed by the Prophet \hon{\PBUH}{\BODY} to Zaynab.

She first married Zayd ibn Ḥārithah, who later divorced her. After the
completion of her *ʿiddah*, the Prophet \hon{\PBUH}{\BODY} married her in Dhū
al-Qaʿdah 5 AH at the age of 35 or 37, with Allah Himself acting as her Walī, as
established in *Sūrat al-Aḥzāb* (Q33:37). Her *mahr* was 400 dirhams. Notably,
Zayd ibn Ḥārithah is the only Companion mentioned by name in the Quran. Her
*walīmah* was the largest among the Prophet's \hon{\PBUH}{\BODY} marriages, attended
by over 300 guests. The verses pertaining to Ḥijāb in *Sūrat al-Nūr* were
revealed regarding this *walīmah*.

She was known for her generosity and charity and remained within her home after
the passing of the Prophet \hon{\PBUH}{\BODY}. She passed away in Madīnah in 20 AH
at the age of 50, and her janāzah prayer was led by ʿUmar ibn al-Khaṭṭāb. She
was the first of the Prophet's \hon{\PBUH}{\BODY} wives to pass away after him and
the first to be buried in al-Baqīʿ al-Gharqad. She narrated 11 *aḥādīth*.

## 8. Juwayriyyah bint al-Ḥārith ([جويرية بنت الحارث]{dir="rtl"})

She belonged to Banū al-Muṣṭaliq and was the daughter of its chieftain,
al-Ḥārith ibn Abī Ḍirār. Her mother was Barrah bint al-Ḥārith.

She had previously been married to Musāfiʿ ibn Ṣafwān, who was killed in battle
against the Muslims. She was originally named Barrah, which the Prophet
\hon{\PBUH}{\BODY} changed to Juwayriyyah, meaning "young girl," reflecting her
youth.

She married the Prophet \hon{\PBUH}{\BODY} in Shaʿbān 6 AH at the age of 19 or 20,
and her *mahr* was her emancipation. Following her marriage, over a hundred
families from her tribe were freed, leading ʿĀʾishah \hon{\P_HER}{\BODY}
to remark, "I do not know of any woman more blessed for her people than
Juwayriyyah."

She passed away in Madīnah in 56 AH at the age of 65, and her janāzah was led by
Marwān ibn al-Ḥakam, the governor of Madinah. She narrated 7 *aḥādīth*.

## 9. Umm Ḥabībah Ramlah bint Abī Sufyān ([رملة بنت ابي سفيان]{dir="rtl"})

She was born in Makkah approximately 10--17 years before Prophethood. She was
the daughter of Abū Sufyān Ṣakhr ibn Ḥarb from Banū Umayyah. Her mother was
Ṣafiyyah bint Abī al-ʿĀṣ. She was also a maternal cousin of ʿUthmān ibn ʿAffān.

She had previously been married to ʿUbaydullāh ibn Jaḥsh, with whom she migrated
to Abyssinia. He later apostatized and died upon Christianity.

She married the Prophet \hon{\PBUH}{\BODY} in 6 AH with a *mahr* of 400 dinars, paid
on his behalf by al-Najāshī. She was 34 or 35 years old. Al-Najāshī invited all
the Muslims in Abyssinia and gave a lavish dinner in honor of this marriage, and
this was the grandest of the Prophet's \hon{\PBUH}{\BODY} *walīmahs*. The marriage
was consummated in Muḥarram 7 AH upon her arrival in Madīnah.

She passed away in Madīnah in 44 AH at the age of 70, during the caliphate of
her brother Muʿāwiyah. She narrated 65 *aḥādīth*.

## 10. Ṣafiyyah bint Ḥuyayy ([صفية بنت حيي]{dir="rtl"})

She was born in Madīnah three years after Prophethood and belonged to Banū
al-Naḍīr. She was the daughter of its chieftain.

She had previously been married to Salām ibn Mishkam and later to Kinānah ibn
Abī al-Ḥuqayq.

She married the Prophet \hon{\PBUH}{\BODY} in Rabīʿ al-Awwal 7 AH at the age of
approximately 16 or 17, and her *mahr* was her emancipation. She was his only
non-Arab wife. When her co-wives remarked about her lineage, the Prophet
\hon{\PBUH}{\BODY} said, "You are the daughter of a prophet, your uncle is a
prophet, and you are married to a prophet." \[Al-Tirmidhī\]

She passed away in Madīnah in 52 AH at the age of 60 during the caliphate of
Muʿāwiyah. She narrated 10 *aḥādīth*.

## 11. Maymūnah bint al-Ḥārith ([ميمونة بنت الحارث]{dir="rtl"})

Her lineage met that of the Prophet's \hon{\PBUH}{\BODY} at Muḍar ibn Nizār, and she
belonged to Banū Hilāl. She was originally named Barrah, which the Prophet
\hon{\PBUH}{\BODY} changed to Maymūnah.

She had previously been married to Abū Rahm ibn ʿAbd al-ʿUzzá, who died upon
polytheism. She accepted Islam in Makkah while the Prophet \hon{\PBUH}{\BODY} was in
Madīnah.

Like Khadījah, she initiated the proposal through her sister Umm al-Faḍl
Lubābah. She married the Prophet \hon{\PBUH}{\BODY} in Dhū al-Qaʿdah 7 AH after
ʿUmrat al-Qaḍāʾ, with a *mahr* of 500 dirhams, at the age of around 35.

She was the sister of Umm al-Faḍl, the wife of al-ʿAbbās, and the uterine sister
of Zaynab bint Khuzaymah. This was the final marriage of the Prophet
\hon{\PBUH}{\BODY}.

Her mother Hind was famously described as "the woman with the most noble
sons-in-law," including the Prophet \hon{\PBUH}{\BODY}, al-ʿAbbās, Ḥamzah, ʿAlī,
Jaʿfar, and Abū Bakr.

She passed away in Wādī Sarif near Makkah in 61 or 63 AH at the age of 81. Her
*janāzah* was led by ʿAbdullāh ibn ʿAbbās. She narrated 76 *aḥādīth*.

Zaynab bint Khuzaymah and Maymūnah bint al-Ḥārith were half-siblings (uterine
sisters). Their common mother was Hind.

The Prophet \hon{\PBUH}{\BODY} didn't marry any other woman while he was married to
Khadījah. After her death, he married Sawdah and didn't live with anyone else
while he was in Makkah. Throughout his life, the maximum number of wives he had
at a time was nine. All of his wives, except ʿĀʾishah, were widowed or divorced
women.

Six of the Prophet's \hon{\PBUH}{\BODY} wives were from the different sub-tribes of
the Quraysh. These were: Khadījah, Sawdah, ʿĀʾishah, Ḥafṣah, Ramlah, and Umm
Salamah. These marriages drew various tribal factions into family ties with him
and lessened their enmity toward him. The other wives were from leading noble
families, which also helped establish political ties in the region and opened
more doors for Daʿwah. Of these, four were from other Arab tribes: Zaynab bint
Jaḥsh, Zaynab bint Khuzaymah, Maymūnah, and Juwayriyyah. His only non-Arab wife
was Ṣafiyyah.

The Prophet \hon{\PBUH}{\BODY} married his wives with a *mahr* of 400--600 dirhams,
which is approximately 1500 grams of silver, and this was the same range for the
*mahr* given to his daughters.

The Prophet \hon{\PBUH}{\BODY} had two concubines:

1. The first was Māriyah al-Qibṭiyyah (a Coptic, Egyptian Christian). She gave
   birth to his son Ibrāhīm, who died in Madīnah in Rabīʿ al-Awwal 10 AH. She
   passed away in Madīnah in the month of Muḥarram in 16 AH. It is reported that
   Māriyah and her sister Sīrīn were daughters of a noble family who had been
   dedicated to the service of the church, similar to Maryam
   \hon{\S_HER}{\BODY}.

2. The second was Rayḥānah bint Zayd al-Naḍriyyah, a captive from Banū Qurayẓah.
   She accepted Islam and chose to remain as a concubine rather than a wife. She
   passed away in Madīnah in the month of Dhū al-Ḥijjah in 10 AH.
