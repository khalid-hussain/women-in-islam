# The Prophet's \hon{\PBUH}{\HEADER} Mother and Aunts {.unnumbered}

## His Mother {.unnumbered}

Her name was Āminah bint Wahb ibn ʿAbd Manāf ibn Zuhrah ibn Kilāb. Her father
was the chief of the Banū Zuhrah tribe. Her mother was Barrah bint ʿAbd al-ʿUzzá
from Banū ʿAbd al-Dār. She passed away at approximately twenty, no more than
thirty, years of age and was buried at al-Abwāʾ (between Makkah and Madīnah)
when Muḥammad \hon{\PBUH}{\BODY} was six years and one month old. His caretaker,
Barakah, then took him back to the care of his grandfather ʿAbd al-Muṭṭalib in
Makkah.

## His Aunts {.unnumbered}

He had six paternal aunts, most of whom were born before their brothers:
Ṣafiyyah, Umm Ḥakīm al-Bayḍāʾ, ʿĀtikah, Umaymah, Arwá, and Barrah. At the advent
of Prophethood, three of them had already passed away. Of the remaining three,
two accepted Islam: Ṣafiyyah and Arwá. Scholars differ regarding the Islam of
ʿĀtikah.

He also had two maternal aunts: Fākhitah bint Wahb and Fāriʿah bint Wahb, both
mentioned in some narrations.

**Arwá:** She was married to ʿUmayr ibn Wahb, with whom she had a son, Ṭulayb.
She accepted Islam. Her son Ṭulayb accepted Islam in Dār al-Arqam, participated
in the Battle of Badr, and was martyred in the Battle of Ajnādayn.

**Ṣafiyyah:** She was married to al-ʿAwwām ibn al-Khuwaylid, the brother of
Khadījah, and had three sons: al-Zubayr, al-Sāʾib, and ʿAbd al-Kaʿbah. ʿAbd
al-Kaʿbah died in childhood, while the others accepted Islam. She passed away in
Madīnah in 20 AH during the caliphate of ʿUmar. She was the full sister of
Ḥamzah and was twenty-three years older than the Prophet \hon{\PBUH}{\BODY}.

**ʿĀtikah:** She was married to Abū Umayyah ibn al-Mughīrah, and had children:
ʿAbdullāh, Zuhayr, and Qarībah. ʿAbdullāh accepted Islam. She is known to have
seen a dream foretelling the losses of Quraysh at Badr. Scholars differ
regarding her acceptance of Islam.

**Umaymah:** She was married to Jaḥsh ibn Riʾāb, and had children: ʿAbdullāh,
Abū Aḥmad, Zaynab, Ḥabībah, and Ḥamnah---all of whom accepted Islam. ʿAbdullāh
was martyred at the Battle of Uḥud, and Zaynab later married the Prophet
\hon{\PBUH}{\BODY}. She also had a son named ʿUbaydullāh, who accepted Islam but
later died a disbeliever in Abyssinia.

**Barrah:** She was married to ʿAbd al-Asad ibn Hilāl, and had a son, Abū
Salamah ʿAbdullāh, who was the first husband of Umm Salamah before she married
the Prophet \hon{\PBUH}{\BODY}.

**Umm Ḥakīm al-Bayḍāʾ:** She was married to Kurayz ibn Rabīʿah and had a
daughter, Arwá, who was the mother of ʿUthmān ibn ʿAffān.

ʿAbdullāh (the Prophet's \hon{\PBUH}{\BODY} father), Abū Ṭālib, al-Zubayr, Umm
Ḥakīm, ʿĀtikah, Umaymah, Arwá, and Barrah were all siblings from the same
mother: Fāṭimah bint ʿAmr.

## His Wet Nurses and Caretakers {.unnumbered}

The first woman to nurse him was his mother, Āminah, who breastfed him for seven
to eight days. Thereafter, he was nursed by Thuwaybah, who also breastfed Ḥamzah
ibn ʿAbd al-Muṭṭalib and Abū Salamah. Thuwaybah passed away in 7 AH. Ḥalīmah
bint Abī Ḍhuʾayb al-Saʿdiyyah then nursed him for two years; She also nursed his
cousin Abū Sufyān al-Ḥārith.

He was further cared for by Shaymāʾ, the daughter of Ḥalīmah and his foster
sister, and most notably by Umm Ayman Barakah bint Thaʿlabah, who played a
central role in his upbringing.

Imam Ibn al-Jawzī \hon{\M_HIM}{\BODY} said, “Ḥalīmah came to see the
Messenger of Allah \hon{\PBUH}{\BODY} twice after she had returned him to his
mother. The first time was after his marriage to Khadījah, during a period of
severe famine in her land. In order to assist her, he spoke to Khadījah, who
gave her one camel and forty goats. When she came the second time, he had
already begun his Prophetic mission. Ḥalīmah accepted Islam and pledged
allegiance to him, and her husband al-Ḥārith also embraced Islam.” \[Subul
al-Hudá wa al-Rashād\]
