# The Prophet's \hon{\PBUH}{\HEADER} Mother and Aunts

**His Mother**

Her name was Āminah bint Wahb ibn ʿAbd Manāf ibn Zuhrah ibn Kilāb. Her father
was the chief of the Banū Zuhrah tribe. Her mother was Barrah bint ʿAbd al-ʿUzzá
from Banū ʿAbd al-Dār. She passed away at approximately twenty, no more than
thirty, years of age and was buried at al-Abwāʾ (between Makkah and Madīnah)
when Muḥammad \hon{\PBUH}{\BODY} was six years and one month old. His caretaker,
Barakah, then took him back to the care of his grandfather ʿAbd al-Muṭṭalib in
Makkah.

**His Aunts**

He had six paternal aunts, most of whom were born before their brothers:
Ṣafiyyah, Umm Ḥakīm al-Bayḍāʾ, ʿĀtikah, Umaymah, Arwá, and Barrah. At the advent
of Prophethood, three of them had already passed away. Of the remaining three,
two accepted Islam: Ṣafiyyah and Arwá. Scholars differ regarding the Islam of
ʿĀtikah.

He also had two maternal aunts: Fākhitah bint Wahb and Fāriʿah bint Wahb, both
mentioned in some narrations.

**Arwá:** She was married to ʿUmayr ibn Wahb, with whom she had a son, Ṭulayb.
She accepted Islam. Her son Ṭulayb accepted Islam in Dār al-Arqam, participated
in the Battle of Badr, and was martyred in the Battle of Ajnādayn.

**Ṣafiyyah:** She was married to al-ʿAwwām ibn al-Khuwaylid, the brother of
Khadījah, and had three sons: al-Zubayr, al-Sāʾib, and ʿAbd al-Kaʿbah. ʿAbd
al-Kaʿbah died in childhood, while the others accepted Islam. She passed away in
Madīnah in 20 AH during the caliphate of ʿUmar. She was the full sister of
Ḥamzah and was twenty-three years older than the Prophet \hon{\PBUH}{\BODY}.

**ʿĀtikah:** She was married to Abū Umayyah ibn al-Mughīrah, and had children:
ʿAbdullāh, Zuhayr, and Qarībah. ʿAbdullāh accepted Islam. She is known to have
seen a dream foretelling the losses of Quraysh at Badr. Scholars differ
regarding her acceptance of Islam.

**Umaymah:** She was married to Jaḥsh ibn Riʾāb, and had children: ʿAbdullāh,
Abū Aḥmad, Zaynab, Ḥabībah, and Ḥamnah---all of whom accepted Islam. ʿAbdullāh
was martyred at the Battle of Uḥud, and Zaynab later married the Prophet
\hon{\PBUH}{\BODY}. She also had a son named ʿUbaydullāh, who accepted Islam but
later died a disbeliever in Abyssinia.

**Barrah:** She was married to ʿAbd al-Asad ibn Hilāl, and had a son, Abū
Salamah ʿAbdullāh, who was the first husband of Umm Salamah before she married
the Prophet \hon{\PBUH}{\BODY}.

**Umm Ḥakīm al-Bayḍāʾ:** She was married to Kurayz ibn Rabīʿah and had a
daughter, Arwá, who was the mother of ʿUthmān ibn ʿAffān.

ʿAbdullāh (the Prophet's \hon{\PBUH}{\BODY} father), Abū Ṭālib, al-Zubayr, Umm
Ḥakīm, ʿĀtikah, Umaymah, Arwá, and Barrah were all siblings from the same
mother: Fāṭimah bint ʿAmr.

**His Wet Nurses and Caretakers**

The first woman to nurse him was his mother, Āminah, who breastfed him for seven
to eight days. Thereafter, he was nursed by Thuwaybah, who also breastfed Ḥamzah
ibn ʿAbd al-Muṭṭalib and Abū Salamah. Thuwaybah passed away in 7 AH. Ḥalīmah
bint Abī Ḍhuʾayb al-Saʿdiyyah then nursed him for two years; She also nursed his
cousin Abū Sufyān al-Ḥārith.

He was further cared for by Shaymāʾ, the daughter of Ḥalīmah and his foster
sister, and most notably by Umm Ayman Barakah bint Thaʿlabah, who played a
central role in his upbringing.

Imam Ibn al-Jawzī \hon{\M_HIM}{\BODY} said, "Ḥalīmah came to see the
Messenger of Allah \hon{\PBUH}{\BODY} twice after she had returned him to his
mother. The first time was after his marriage to Khadījah, during a period of
severe famine in her land. In order to assist her, he spoke to Khadījah, who
gave her one camel and forty goats. When she came the second time, he had
already begun his Prophetic mission. Ḥalīmah accepted Islam and pledged
allegiance to him, and her husband al-Ḥārith also embraced Islam." \[Subul
al-Hudá wa al-Rashād\]

# Ahl al-Bayt: Wives of the Prophet \hon{\PBUH}{\BODY}

An easy way to memorize their names is through the phrase:

[خَسٌّ مَعَ جَزَرٍ صَحَّة]{dir="rtl"}ٌ

Lettuce with carrots is healthy.

## 1. Khadījah bint Khuwaylid ([خديجة بنت خويلد]{dir="rtl"})

Her lineage met that of the Prophet's \hon{\PBUH}{\BODY} at Quṣayy ibn Kilāb. She
belonged to the Banū Asad sub-tribe of Quraysh. Her father, Khuwaylid, was a
close associate of ʿAbd al-Muṭṭalib and stood alongside him during the incident
of Abraha's attack on Makkah. Her mother was Fāṭimah bint Zāʾidah from Banū
ʿĀmir. She was famously known as al-Ṭāhirah.

Khadījah had previously been married to ʿAtīq ibn ʿĀʾidh al-Makhzūmī, with whom
she had a daughter named Hind. After his death, she married Abū Hālah Hind ibn
Zurārah al-Tamīmī, with whom she had children: Hind, Hālah, Ḥārith, and Ṭāhir.

Her friend Nafīsah approached the Prophet \hon{\PBUH}{\BODY} with her proposal, and
thereafter, Ḥamzah ibn ʿAbd al-Muṭṭalib approached her uncle ʿAmr ibn Asad on
behalf of the Prophet \hon{\PBUH}{\BODY}. She was approximately 28 years old at the
time of marriage, though some scholars state she was 40. This took place 25
years before the Hijrah.

The Prophet \hon{\PBUH}{\BODY} married her at the age of 25, giving a *mahr* of
either 20 young camels or, as reported in other narrations, 12.5 *ūqiyyah*
(approximately 500 dirhams) of silver. The marriage is reported to have taken
place about three months after his return from Shām, toward the end of the month
of Ṣafar. A cow was slaughtered by her family, and Abū Ṭālib delivered a sermon.
The Prophet \hon{\PBUH}{\BODY} also slaughtered camels to feed the guests.

She was the mother of all the children of the Prophet \hon{\PBUH}{\BODY} except
Ibrāhīm. The Prophet \hon{\PBUH}{\BODY} said, "Indeed, Allah did not grant me better
than her; she accepted me when people rejected me, she believed in me when
people doubted me, she shared her wealth with me when people deprived me, and
Allah granted me children only through her." \[Ṣaḥīḥ Muslim\]

She was the first to hear the revelation from the Prophet \hon{\PBUH}{\BODY}, the
first to perform *wuḍūʾ* and pray with him, and the first to believe in his
message. The Prophet \hon{\PBUH}{\BODY} said, "Her love was nurtured in my heart by
Allah Himself." \[Ṣaḥīḥ Muslim\]

He did not marry any other woman during her lifetime. She was the first person
to be given the glad tidings of Jannah, and she was given specific glad tidings
of a palace in Jannah. The Messenger of Allah \hon{\PBUH}{\BODY} conveyed to her the
Salām from Allah and from Jibrīl [عليه السلام]{dir="rtl"}.

She passed away in Makkah in Ramaḍān, three years before the Hijrah, at the age
of either 53 or 65. She was buried in al-Ḥajūn by the Prophet \hon{\PBUH}{\BODY}.
Ṣalāt al-Janāzah had not yet been prescribed at that time.

The Prophet \hon{\PBUH}{\BODY} said, "The best woman of her time was Maryam bint
ʿImrān, and the best woman of this Ummah is Khadījah." \[Ṣaḥīḥ al-Bukhārī\]

## 2. Sawdah bint Zamʿah ([سودة بنت زمعة]{dir="rtl"})

Her lineage met that of the Prophet's \hon{\PBUH}{\BODY} at Luʾayy ibn Ghālib. She
was born in Makkah and was the daughter of Zamʿah ibn Qays from Banū ʿĀmir. Her
mother was al-Shamūs bint Qays from Banū al-Najjār.

She was previously married to al-Sakrān ibn ʿAmr, with whom she had a son, ʿAbd
al-Raḥmān. Together, they migrated twice to Abyssinia. Her mother was also a
cousin of ʿAbd al-Muṭṭalib.

While in Abyssinia, she saw two dreams which were interpreted as indicating her
future marriage to the Prophet \hon{\PBUH}{\BODY}. She later married the Prophet
\hon{\PBUH}{\BODY} in Ramaḍān, in the tenth year of his Prophethood, with a *mahr*
of 400 dirhams. She was approximately 50--55 years old at the time. For three
years, she remained his only wife after the passing of Khadījah [رضي الله
عنها]{dir="rtl"}.

The *āyāt* pertaining to Ḥijāb in *Sūrat al-Nūr* were revealed in connection
with her. After the passing of the Prophet \hon{\PBUH}{\BODY}, she chose to remain
within her home and did not go out.

She passed away in Madīnah in Shawwāl 23 AH, at the age of 72, during the
caliphate of ʿUmar ibn al-Khaṭṭāb. She narrated five *aḥādīth* from the Prophet
\hon{\PBUH}{\BODY}.

## 3. ʿĀʾishah bint Abī Bakr ([عائشة بنت أبي بكر]{dir="rtl"})

Her lineage met that of the Prophet's \hon{\PBUH}{\BODY} at Luʾayy ibn Ghālib. She
was the daughter of Abū Bakr al-Ṣiddīq and belonged to the Banū Taym sub-tribe
of Quraysh. Her mother was Umm Rummān Zaynab bint ʿĀmir from Banū Kinānah.

She was born in Makkah approximately four to five years after the beginning of
Prophethood. She was the only virgin whom the Prophet \hon{\PBUH}{\BODY} married.
She was shown to the Prophet \hon{\PBUH}{\BODY} in a dream by Jibrīl [عليه
السلام]{dir="rtl"} as his future wife.

She married the Prophet \hon{\PBUH}{\BODY} in Shawwāl in the tenth year of
Prophethood, with a *mahr* of 400 dirhams, at the age of six. The marriage was
consummated when she was nine, and she moved into his household in Shawwāl 1 AH.
She was approximately five to eight years younger than Fāṭimah, and she was born
into Islam.

At the time of the Prophet's \hon{\PBUH}{\BODY} marriage to ʿĀʾishah [رضي الله
عنها]{dir="rtl"}, even the pagan Makkans who were seeking to slander him did not
raise objections to this marriage, while they did raise objections to the
marriage with Zaynab. In reality, ʿĀʾishah was already engaged to Muṭʿim ibn
ʿAdī before this. That was the norm of the time, and women attained puberty
faster because of the climate. For over a 1000 years, the Muslim Ummah has had
no issues with the age of ʿĀʾishah.

ʿĀʾishah \hon{\P_HER}{\BODY} said, "If a girl (*jāriyah*) were to reach
puberty at the age of nine, then she is a woman." \[Al-Tirmidhī\]

Imam Ibn Ḥazm \hon{\M_HIM}{\BODY} said, "The age of ʿĀʾishah is recorded in
the texts without a difference of opinion." \[Ḥajjat al-Widāʿ\]

Imam Ibn Kathīr \hon{\M_HIM}{\BODY} said, "This is a matter in which there is
no difference of opinion amongst people." \[Al-Sīrah al-Nabawiyyah\]

Imam Ibn ʿAbd al-Barr \hon{\M_HIM}{\BODY} said, "I do not know of anyone
differing on this." \[Al-Istīʿāb\]

She was the only wife whose parents migrated to Madīnah. Revelation would
descend upon the Prophet \hon{\PBUH}{\BODY} while he was in her company. The rulings
of *tayammum* were revealed in 4 AH in connection with her.

She was around twelve years old at the time of the Incident of Ifk, which
occurred after the expedition of al-Muraysīʿ, before the Battle of al-Aḥzāb and
the events of Banū Qurayẓah in 5 AH.

The Prophet \hon{\PBUH}{\BODY} affectionately called her "Ḥumayrāʾ" and would
shorten her name. She reported, "Once the Messenger of Allah \hon{\PBUH}{\BODY} said
to me, 'O ʿĀʾish! This is Jibrīl greeting you.'"

*Āyāt* of the Quran were revealed, declaring her innocence---to be recited until
the Day of Judgment. She also witnessed Jibrīl [عليه السلام]{dir="rtl"} in a
non-angelic form.

The Prophet \hon{\PBUH}{\BODY} passed away while leaning against her chest when she
was eighteen years old. She became one of the greatest scholars of the Ummah.
Imam al-Ḥākim stated, "One-fourth of the rulings of the Sharīʿah were narrated
on the authority of ʿĀʾishah."

She passed away in Madīnah on 17 Ramaḍān 58 AH at the age of 66. Her *janāzah*
was led by Abū Hurayrah. She narrated 2,210 *aḥādīth* from the Prophet
\hon{\PBUH}{\BODY}.

The Prophet \hon{\PBUH}{\BODY} said, "Many men attained perfection, but among women
only Āsiyah, the wife of Firʿawn, and Maryam bint ʿImrān attained perfection.
And the superiority of ʿĀʾishah over other women is like the superiority of
*tharīd* over all other foods." \[Ṣaḥīḥ al-Bukhārī\]

## 4. Ḥafṣah bint ʿUmar ([حفصة بنت عمر]{dir="rtl"})

Her lineage met that of the Prophet's \hon{\PBUH}{\BODY} at Kaʿb ibn Luʾayy. She was
the daughter of ʿUmar ibn al-Khaṭṭāb and belonged to the Banū ʿAdī sub-tribe of
Quraysh. Her mother was Zaynab bint Maẓʿūn from Banū Jumah.

She was born in Makkah approximately five years before Prophethood, around
eighteen years before the Hijrah, and was close in age to Fāṭimah.

She was previously married at the age of 14 to Khuṇays ibn Ḥudhāfah, who was one
of the first twenty people to accept Islam. She migrated to Madinah along with
her father and husband. Khunais was the only participant of his tribe, the Banu
Sahm, at Badr, and was martyred after sustaining a wound at Uhud.

It is reported that she accepted Islam before her father due to her marriage to
Khuṇays. She later married the Prophet \hon{\PBUH}{\BODY} in Shaʿbān 3 AH with a
*mahr* of 400 dirhams at the age of nineteen.

Her worship was praised by the Angel Jibrīl [عليه السلام]{dir="rtl"}, who said
to the Prophet \hon{\PBUH}{\BODY}, "She is one who frequently fasts and prays, and
she will be your wife in Paradise."

She was the custodian of the first compiled *muṣḥaf* of the Quran during the
caliphate of Abū Bakr. She passed away in Madīnah in 45 AH at approximately
sixty years of age. Her janāzah prayer was led by Marwān ibn al-Ḥakam, the
governor of Madīnah. She narrated 60 *aḥādīth* from the Prophet \hon{\PBUH}{\BODY}.

## 5. Zaynab bint Khuzaymah ([زينب بنت خزيمة]{dir="rtl"})

Her lineage met that of the Prophet's \hon{\PBUH}{\BODY} at ʿAbd Manāf. She belonged
to the tribe of Banū Hilāl. Her father was Khuzaymah ibn al-Ḥārith, and her
mother was Hind bint ʿAwf.

She was born in Makkah approximately thirteen years before the Hijrah. She was
previously married to Ṭufayl ibn al-Ḥārith ibn ʿAbd al-Muṭṭalib, and later to
his brother ʿUbaydah ibn al-Ḥārith. He was the first to lead an expedition under
Islam, and he was martyred from a wound he got at Badr. ʿUbaydah was one of the
first martyrs in Islam. Reports that she later married ʿAbdullāh ibn Jaḥsh are
considered weak.

She married the Prophet \hon{\PBUH}{\BODY} in Ramaḍān 3 AH with a *mahr* of 400
dirhams at around 28--29 years of age. There were approximately twenty days
between her marriage and that of Ḥafṣah. She was the first non-Qurashī wife of
the Prophet \hon{\PBUH}{\BODY}.

She was known as Umm al-Masākīn due to her compassion and generosity toward the
poor. She was also the maternal (uterine) sister of Maymūnah bint al-Ḥārith.

She passed away in Madīnah in Rabīʿ al-Awwal 4 AH at the age of 30. The Prophet
\hon{\PBUH}{\BODY} himself led her *janāzah* prayer. She was the only wife for whom
he performed *janāzah* and the last of his wives to pass away during his
lifetime.

She remained married to the Prophet \hon{\PBUH}{\BODY} for only two to three months,
though some reports extend this to eight months, placing her passing in Rabīʿ
al-Ākhir 4 AH.

## 6. Umm Salamah Hind bint Abī Umayyah ([هند بنت أبي أُميّة]{dir="rtl"})

Her lineage met that of the Prophet's \hon{\PBUH}{\BODY} at Murrah ibn Kaʿb. She was
born in Makkah and was the daughter of Abū Umayyah ibn al-Mughīrah from Banū
Makhzūm. Her mother was ʿĀtikah bint ʿĀmir from Banū Kinānah.

She was the foster sister of ʿAmmār ibn Yāsir. She was previously married to Abū
Salamah ʿAbdullāh ibn ʿAbd al-Asad, with whom she had children: Salamah, Durrah,
ʿUmar, and Zaynab. Salamah was the first child born to the Muslims in Abyssinia.

She and her husband were among the earliest to migrate---twice to Abyssinia and
later to Madīnah. Abū Salamah, who was also the foster brother of the Prophet
\hon{\PBUH}{\BODY}, passed away from wounds sustained at Uḥud.

Umm Salamah reported, "I heard the Messenger of Allah \hon{\PBUH}{\BODY} saying,
'When a person suffers from a calamity and utters, "We belong to Allah and to
Him we shall return. O Allah! Compensate me in my affliction, recompense my loss
and give me something better in exchange for it," then Allah surely compensates
him with reward and a better substitute.'" Umm Salamah said, "When Abū Salamah
died, I repeated the same supplication as the Messenger of Allah \hon{\PBUH}{\BODY}
had commanded me with. So Allah bestowed upon me a better substitute than him."
\[Ṣaḥīḥ Muslim\]

She married the Prophet \hon{\PBUH}{\BODY} in Shawwāl 4 AH at the age of 29. After
marriage, she moved into the residence of Zaynab bint Khuzaymah. Her daughter,
originally named Barrah, was renamed Zaynab by the Prophet \hon{\PBUH}{\BODY}.

Several verses of the Quran were revealed in response to her questions. She also
witnessed the Angel Jibrīl [عليه السلام]{dir="rtl"} in a non-angelic form. She
was the only Mother of the Believers alive at the time of the martyrdom of
Ḥusayn at Karbalāʾ.

She passed away in Madīnah in Shawwāl 62 AH at approximately 84 years of age.
Her *janāzah* was led by Abū Hurayrah. She was the last of the Prophet's
\hon{\PBUH}{\BODY} wives to pass away. She narrated 378 *aḥādīth*, making her the
second-highest narrator among the Mothers of the Believers after ʿĀʾishah.

## 7. Zaynab bint Jaḥsh ([زينب بنت جحش]{dir="rtl"})

Her lineage met that of the Prophet's \hon{\PBUH}{\BODY} at Khuzaymah ibn Mudrikah.
She was born in Makkah and was the daughter of Jaḥsh ibn Riʾāb from Banū Asad.
Her mother was Umaymah bint ʿAbd al-Muṭṭalib from Banū Hāshim, making her the
cousin of the Prophet \hon{\PBUH}{\BODY} and the closest to him in lineage among his
wives.

She was born seventeen years before Prophethood and was originally named Barrah,
a name later changed by the Prophet \hon{\PBUH}{\BODY} to Zaynab.

She first married Zayd ibn Ḥārithah, who later divorced her. After the
completion of her *ʿiddah*, the Prophet \hon{\PBUH}{\BODY} married her in Dhū
al-Qaʿdah 5 AH at the age of 35 or 37, with Allah Himself acting as her Walī, as
established in *Sūrat al-Aḥzāb* (Q33:37). Her *mahr* was 400 dirhams. Notably,
Zayd ibn Ḥārithah is the only Companion mentioned by name in the Quran. Her
*walīmah* was the largest among the Prophet's \hon{\PBUH}{\BODY} marriages, attended
by over 300 guests. The verses pertaining to Ḥijāb in *Sūrat al-Nūr* were
revealed regarding this *walīmah*.

She was known for her generosity and charity and remained within her home after
the passing of the Prophet \hon{\PBUH}{\BODY}. She passed away in Madīnah in 20 AH
at the age of 50, and her janāzah prayer was led by ʿUmar ibn al-Khaṭṭāb. She
was the first of the Prophet's \hon{\PBUH}{\BODY} wives to pass away after him and
the first to be buried in al-Baqīʿ al-Gharqad. She narrated 11 *aḥādīth*.

## 8. Juwayriyyah bint al-Ḥārith ([جويرية بنت الحارث]{dir="rtl"})

She belonged to Banū al-Muṣṭaliq and was the daughter of its chieftain,
al-Ḥārith ibn Abī Ḍirār. Her mother was Barrah bint al-Ḥārith.

She had previously been married to Musāfiʿ ibn Ṣafwān, who was killed in battle
against the Muslims. She was originally named Barrah, which the Prophet
\hon{\PBUH}{\BODY} changed to Juwayriyyah, meaning "young girl," reflecting her
youth.

She married the Prophet \hon{\PBUH}{\BODY} in Shaʿbān 6 AH at the age of 19 or 20,
and her *mahr* was her emancipation. Following her marriage, over a hundred
families from her tribe were freed, leading ʿĀʾishah \hon{\P_HER}{\BODY}
to remark, "I do not know of any woman more blessed for her people than
Juwayriyyah."

She passed away in Madīnah in 56 AH at the age of 65, and her janāzah was led by
Marwān ibn al-Ḥakam, the governor of Madinah. She narrated 7 *aḥādīth*.

## 9. Umm Ḥabībah Ramlah bint Abī Sufyān ([رملة بنت ابي سفيان]{dir="rtl"})

She was born in Makkah approximately 10--17 years before Prophethood. She was
the daughter of Abū Sufyān Ṣakhr ibn Ḥarb from Banū Umayyah. Her mother was
Ṣafiyyah bint Abī al-ʿĀṣ. She was also a maternal cousin of ʿUthmān ibn ʿAffān.

She had previously been married to ʿUbaydullāh ibn Jaḥsh, with whom she migrated
to Abyssinia. He later apostatized and died upon Christianity.

She married the Prophet \hon{\PBUH}{\BODY} in 6 AH with a *mahr* of 400 dinars, paid
on his behalf by al-Najāshī. She was 34 or 35 years old. Al-Najāshī invited all
the Muslims in Abyssinia and gave a lavish dinner in honor of this marriage, and
this was the grandest of the Prophet's \hon{\PBUH}{\BODY} *walīmahs*. The marriage
was consummated in Muḥarram 7 AH upon her arrival in Madīnah.

She passed away in Madīnah in 44 AH at the age of 70, during the caliphate of
her brother Muʿāwiyah. She narrated 65 *aḥādīth*.

## 10. Ṣafiyyah bint Ḥuyayy ([صفية بنت حيي]{dir="rtl"})

She was born in Madīnah three years after Prophethood and belonged to Banū
al-Naḍīr. She was the daughter of its chieftain.

She had previously been married to Salām ibn Mishkam and later to Kinānah ibn
Abī al-Ḥuqayq.

She married the Prophet \hon{\PBUH}{\BODY} in Rabīʿ al-Awwal 7 AH at the age of
approximately 16 or 17, and her *mahr* was her emancipation. She was his only
non-Arab wife. When her co-wives remarked about her lineage, the Prophet
\hon{\PBUH}{\BODY} said, "You are the daughter of a prophet, your uncle is a
prophet, and you are married to a prophet." \[Al-Tirmidhī\]

She passed away in Madīnah in 52 AH at the age of 60 during the caliphate of
Muʿāwiyah. She narrated 10 *aḥādīth*.

## 11. Maymūnah bint al-Ḥārith ([ميمونة بنت الحارث]{dir="rtl"})

Her lineage met that of the Prophet's \hon{\PBUH}{\BODY} at Muḍar ibn Nizār, and she
belonged to Banū Hilāl. She was originally named Barrah, which the Prophet
\hon{\PBUH}{\BODY} changed to Maymūnah.

She had previously been married to Abū Rahm ibn ʿAbd al-ʿUzzá, who died upon
polytheism. She accepted Islam in Makkah while the Prophet \hon{\PBUH}{\BODY} was in
Madīnah.

Like Khadījah, she initiated the proposal through her sister Umm al-Faḍl
Lubābah. She married the Prophet \hon{\PBUH}{\BODY} in Dhū al-Qaʿdah 7 AH after
ʿUmrat al-Qaḍāʾ, with a *mahr* of 500 dirhams, at the age of around 35.

She was the sister of Umm al-Faḍl, the wife of al-ʿAbbās, and the uterine sister
of Zaynab bint Khuzaymah. This was the final marriage of the Prophet
\hon{\PBUH}{\BODY}.

Her mother Hind was famously described as "the woman with the most noble
sons-in-law," including the Prophet \hon{\PBUH}{\BODY}, al-ʿAbbās, Ḥamzah, ʿAlī,
Jaʿfar, and Abū Bakr.

She passed away in Wādī Sarif near Makkah in 61 or 63 AH at the age of 81. Her
*janāzah* was led by ʿAbdullāh ibn ʿAbbās. She narrated 76 *aḥādīth*.

Zaynab bint Khuzaymah and Maymūnah bint al-Ḥārith were half-siblings (uterine
sisters). Their common mother was Hind.

---

The Prophet \hon{\PBUH}{\BODY} didn't marry any other woman while he was married to
Khadījah. After her death, he married Sawdah and didn't live with anyone else
while he was in Makkah. Throughout his life, the maximum number of wives he had
at a time was nine. All of his wives, except ʿĀʾishah, were widowed or divorced
women.

Six of the Prophet's \hon{\PBUH}{\BODY} wives were from the different sub-tribes of
the Quraysh. These were: Khadījah, Sawdah, ʿĀʾishah, Ḥafṣah, Ramlah, and Umm
Salamah. These marriages drew various tribal factions into family ties with him
and lessened their enmity toward him. The other wives were from leading noble
families, which also helped establish political ties in the region and opened
more doors for Daʿwah. Of these, four were from other Arab tribes: Zaynab bint
Jaḥsh, Zaynab bint Khuzaymah, Maymūnah, and Juwayriyyah. His only non-Arab wife
was Ṣafiyyah.

The Prophet \hon{\PBUH}{\BODY} married his wives with a *mahr* of 400--600 dirhams,
which is approximately 1500 grams of silver, and this was the same range for the
*mahr* given to his daughters.

The Prophet \hon{\PBUH}{\BODY} had two concubines:

1. The first was Māriyah al-Qibṭiyyah (a Coptic, Egyptian Christian). She gave
   birth to his son Ibrāhīm, who died in Madīnah in Rabīʿ al-Awwal 10 AH. She
   passed away in Madīnah in the month of Muḥarram in 16 AH. It is reported that
   Māriyah and her sister Sīrīn were daughters of a noble family who had been
   dedicated to the service of the church, similar to Maryam
   \hon{\S_HER}{\BODY}.

2. The second was Rayḥānah bint Zayd al-Naḍriyyah, a captive from Banū Qurayẓah.
   She accepted Islam and chose to remain as a concubine rather than a wife. She
   passed away in Madīnah in the month of Dhū al-Ḥijjah in 10 AH.

# Ahl al-Bayt: The Prophet's \hon{\PBUH}{\BODY} Daughters

All the Prophet's \hon{\PBUH}{\BODY} daughters were from his first wife, Khadījah
\hon{\P_HER}{\BODY}. In order of their birth, the daughters of the
Prophet \hon{\PBUH}{\BODY} were:

## 1. Zaynab bint Muḥammad ([زينب بنت محمد]{dir="rtl"})

She was the eldest daughter of the Prophet \hon{\PBUH}{\BODY}. She was born in
Makkah, ten years before Prophethood, when the Prophet \hon{\PBUH}{\BODY} was thirty
years old.

She was married to Abū al-ʿĀṣ ibn al-Rabīʿ, her maternal cousin, the son of
Hālah bint Khuwaylid ibn Asad. She had a son named ʿAlī and a daughter named
Umāmah. She also had another daughter in Madīnah who passed away in infancy.

At the beginning of her *hijrah*, she was attacked by the pagans, causing her to
fall from her camel onto a rock, which resulted in severe injuries. ʿAlī ibn Abī
al-ʿĀṣ passed away during her lifetime. At the Conquest of Makkah, the Prophet
\hon{\PBUH}{\BODY} entered the city on a riding animal, with ʿAlī seated behind him.

The Prophet \hon{\PBUH}{\BODY} would lead the Ṣalāh while carrying Umāmah on his
shoulders. She was described as "the most beloved family member" when the
Prophet \hon{\PBUH}{\BODY} gifted her an expensive necklace. Later, Umāmah married
ʿAlī ibn Abī Ṭālib in 12 AH under the supervision of al-Zubayr ibn al-ʿAwwām, as
per the bequest of Zaynab. After the martyrdom of ʿAlī, she married Mughīrah ibn
Nawfal ibn al-Ḥārith ibn ʿAbd al-Muṭṭalib.

Zaynab died as a martyr in Madīnah in 8 AH at the age of 30 due to complications
from her earlier injuries. She was washed by Sawdah bint Zamʿah, Umm Salamah,
Umm Ayman, and Umm ʿAṭiyyah. Her shroud consisted of garments worn by the
Prophet \hon{\PBUH}{\BODY} that day. She was the first woman for whom a bier was
constructed.

The Prophet \hon{\PBUH}{\BODY} made *duʿāʾ* for ease in her grave and personally led
her *janāzah*.

## 2. Ruqayyah bint Muḥammad ([رقية بنت محمد]{dir="rtl"})

She was born in Makkah, eight years before Prophethood, when the Prophet
\hon{\PBUH}{\BODY} was thirty-two years old -- approximately three years after
Zaynab.

She was initially married to ʿUtbah ibn Abī Lahab, though the marriage was not
consummated. Following the revelation of *Sūrat al-Lahab*, she was divorced. The
Prophet \hon{\PBUH}{\BODY} then married her to ʿUthmān ibn ʿAffān. The women of
Quraysh would praise them in poetry as the most beautiful couple.

She migrated with ʿUthmān to Abyssinia in the first wave of migration. They were
the first couple to migrate after Prophet Lūṭ [عليه السلام]{dir="rtl"}. They
were blessed with a son, ʿAbdullāh, in Abyssinia, the only grandchild of the
Prophet \hon{\PBUH}{\BODY} born outside Arabia. She later returned to Makkah and
then migrated again to Madīnah.

Her son ʿAbdullāh passed away in Madīnah at the age of six in 4 AH. She had also
suffered a miscarriage earlier in Abyssinia.

The Prophet \hon{\PBUH}{\BODY} equated ʿUthmān's staying back to care for her with
participation in the Battle of Badr. She passed away in Madīnah in Ramaḍān 2 AH
at the age of 22. The Prophet \hon{\PBUH}{\BODY} made *duʿāʾ* for her and led her
*janāzah*.

## 3. Umm Kulthūm bint Muḥammad ([أم كلثوم بنت محمد]{dir="rtl"})

She was born in Makkah, seven years before Prophethood, when the Prophet
\hon{\PBUH}{\BODY} was thirty-three years old.

She was initially married to ʿUtaybah ibn Abī Lahab, though the marriage was not
consummated. Following the revelation of *Sūrat al-Lahab*, she was divorced. The
Prophet \hon{\PBUH}{\BODY} then married her to ʿUthmān ibn ʿAffān in Rabīʿ al-Awwal
3 AH.

She migrated to Madīnah along with the families of the Prophet \hon{\PBUH}{\BODY}
and Abū Bakr. She did not have any children.

She passed away in Madīnah in Shaʿbān 9 AH at the age of 27. She was washed by
Ṣafiyyah bint ʿAbd al-Muṭṭalib, Asmāʾ bint ʿUmays, Laylá al-Thaqafiyyah, and Umm
ʿAṭiyyah.

The Prophet \hon{\PBUH}{\BODY} made *duʿāʾ* for her and led her *janāzah*.

## 4. Fāṭimah bint Muḥammad ([فاطمة بنت محمد]{dir="rtl"})

She was born in Makkah, five years before Prophethood, when the Prophet
\hon{\PBUH}{\BODY} was thirty-five years old. Some scholars state she was born in
the very year of Prophethood.

She migrated to Madīnah with the family of the Prophet \hon{\PBUH}{\BODY} and Abū
Bakr.

She married ʿAlī ibn Abī Ṭālib in Rajab 1 AH. Her *mahr* was a Ḥuṭamī armor,
valued at 400--500 dirhams. The marriage was consummated after the Battle of
Badr in 2 AH, when she was approximately 15--18 years old.

She supported the Muslim army during the Battle of Uḥud by providing water and
medical aid. She closely resembled the Prophet \hon{\PBUH}{\BODY} in her walk,
personality, and character.

She had three sons: Ḥasan, Ḥusayn, and Muḥsin, and daughters: Zaynab, Umm
Kulthūm, and, according to some reports, Ruqayyah. Muḥsin and Ruqayyah passed
away in infancy.

Umm Kulthūm bint ʿAlī later married ʿUmar ibn al-Khaṭṭāb in 17 AH, and Zaynab
bint ʿAlī married ʿAbdullāh ibn Jaʿfar ibn Abī Ṭālib.

She passed away in Madīnah in Ramaḍān 11 AH at the age of 29. She was the last
of the Prophet's \hon{\PBUH}{\BODY} children to pass away. She was washed by Asmāʾ
bint ʿUmays, Salmah, and Umm Ayman, under the supervision of ʿAlī ibn Abī Ṭālib.

She was buried in Madīnah, and her janāzah prayer was led by Abū Bakr al-Ṣiddīq.
She narrated 18 *aḥādīth* from the Prophet \hon{\PBUH}{\BODY}.

The Prophet \hon{\PBUH}{\BODY} said, "Indeed, this is an angel who had not descended
to the earth before this night. He sought permission from his Lord to greet me
and give me glad tidings that Fāṭimah will be the queen of the women of Jannah,
and that Ḥasan and Ḥusayn will be the leaders of the youth of Jannah."
\[Al-Tirmidhī\]

ʿĀʾishah \hon{\P_HER}{\BODY} reported that the Prophet \hon{\PBUH}{\BODY}
said, "The queens of the women of Jannah are four: Maryam bint ʿImrān, Fāṭimah
bint Rasūlillāh, Khadījah bint Khuwaylid, and Āsiyah, the wife of Firʿawn."
\[Al-Mustadrak\]

All four attained their lofty ranks through unwavering *īmān* and servitude to
Allah, excelling as daughters, wives, and mothers. Āsiyah and Maryam nurtured
great Prophets, while Khadījah and Fāṭimah were the best of wives to their
husbands. Fāṭimah was the best of her father\'s daughters.

All of the Prophet's \hon{\PBUH}{\BODY} children passed away during his lifetime
except Fāṭimah \hon{\P_HER}{\BODY}, who passed away six months after his
death. Some scholars of Sīrah have differed regarding whether Fāṭimah [رضي الله
عنها]{dir="rtl"} or Umm Kulthūm \hon{\P_HER}{\BODY} was the last daughter
to be born.

A distinctive quality of the Prophet's \hon{\PBUH}{\BODY} daughters is that none of
them were ever co-wives.

- ʿUthmān ibn ʿAffān was married only to Ruqayyah \hon{\P_HER}{\BODY}
  during her lifetime.

- ʿUthmān ibn ʿAffān was married only to Umm Kulthūm \hon{\P_HER}{\BODY}
  during her lifetime. He had other wives after her passing.

- Similarly, ʿAlī ibn Abī Ṭālib was married only to Fāṭimah [رضي الله
  عنها]{dir="rtl"} during her lifetime, and only married other women after her
  passing.

## Stepdaughters of the Prophet \hon{\PBUH}{\HEADER}

The Prophet \hon{\PBUH}{\BODY} had fifteen stepchildren. Of these, he had four
stepdaughters:

1. Hind bint ʿAtīq from Khadījah \hon{\P_HER}{\BODY}.

2. Durrah bint Abī Salamah from Umm Salamah \hon{\P_HER}{\BODY}.

3. Zaynab bint Abī Salamah from Umm Salamah \hon{\P_HER}{\BODY}.

4. Ḥabībah bint ʿUbaydullāh from Ramlah (Umm Ḥabībah) \hon{\P_HER}{\BODY}.

In addition to his own family and stepchildren, the Prophet \hon{\PBUH}{\BODY}
also took personal responsibility for the care and upbringing of several orphan
children. Among the most notable examples are the three orphan daughters of
Asʿad ibn Zurārah \hon{\P_HIM}{\BODY}: al-Fāriʿah, Ḥabībah, and Kabshah. These
young girls were raised within the household of the Prophet \hon{\PBUH}{\BODY},
living among his noble wives and under his direct care and supervision. He
ensured not only their upbringing but also personally arranged their marriages
to suitable and reputable suitors, reflecting his deep commitment to the
welfare, dignity, and protection of the vulnerable in society.

# The Marriage of Fatimah and Ali \hon{\P_TWO}{\HEADER}

It was narrated from ʿAbdullāh ibn Buraydah that his father said, “Abū Bakr and
ʿUmar \hon{\P_TWO}{\BODY} proposed marriage to Fāṭimah, but the Messenger of
Allah said, ‘She is young.’ Then ʿAlī proposed marriage to her, and he married
her to him.” \[Al-Nasāʾī\]

Imam Ibn Saʿd \hon{\M_HIM}{\BODY} recorded, “ʿAlī ibn Abī Ṭālib
\hon{\P_HIM}{\BODY} married Fāṭimah \hon{\P_HER}{\BODY} in Rajab (1 AH), 5
months after the Prophet\'s \hon{\PBUH}{\BODY} *hijrah* to Madīnah. He
consummated his marriage with her after he returned from the Battle of Badr (in
Ramadan 2 AH), and she was 18 years old when he consummated the marriage with
her.” \[Al-Ṭabaqāt al-Kubrá\] Imam ʿAlī al-Ḥalabī \hon{\M_HIM}{\BODY} writes,
“ʿAlī \hon{\P_HIM}{\BODY} was 21 years old when he married Fāṭimah [رضي الله
عنها]{dir="rtl"}, and that she was 15 years old.” \[Al-Sīrah al-Ḥalabīyyah\]

ʿAlī \hon{\P_HIM}{\BODY} said, “I wanted to propose marriage to the daughter of
the Messenger of Allah \hon{\PBUH}{\BODY}, but I thought, ‘I have nothing, so
how could it be?’ Then I remembered how he upheld ties of kinship and his
kindness and generosity, so I asked him for his daughter's hand in marriage. He
said, ‘Do you have anything?’ I said, ‘No.’ He said, ‘Where is the Hutamiyyah
shield that I gave you on such and such a day?’ I said, ‘I have it.’ He said,
‘Then give it to her.’” \[Aḥmad\]

Fāṭimah \hon{\P_HER}{\BODY} was married to ʿAlī ibn Abī Ṭālib
\hon{\P_HIM}{\BODY} at the age of 15 with a *mahr* of a Hutami armor that was
worth 400--500 dirhams after the Battle of Badr in 2 AH. ʿAlī was 10 years older
than her. After the nikah, Fāṭimah \hon{\P_HER}{\BODY} walked to the house of
ʿAlī with Umm Ayman. She was around 15 years and 5 months of age at this time,
while ʿAlī was around 21 or 25 years of age. \[Tafsīr al-Qurṭubī\] Saʿd ibn
Muʿādh \hon{\P_HIM}{\BODY} sacrificed his camel to organise their *walīmah*.

The Prophet \hon{\PBUH}{\BODY} commanded ʿĀʾishah \hon{\P_HER}{\BODY} to
decorate the house for Fāṭimah \hon{\P_HER}{\BODY}. Umm Salamah
\hon{\P_HER}{\BODY} assisted ʿĀʾishah in this task. ʿĀʾishah reports that they
began preparations and brought good-quality sand from Wadī Batha', with which
they plastered the walls. They then cleaned and straightened the barks of the
date-palm tree and made two cushions out of them, and prepared a dish with dates
and raisins and a drink of sweet water. They then placed a stick in the corner
of the room to hang clothes and a leather water-skin. When these arrangements
were completed, ʿĀʾishah \hon{\P_HER}{\BODY} commented, "We never saw a marriage
more exquisite than that of Fatimah." \[Ibn Mājah\]

ʿAlī ibn Abī Ṭālib \hon{\P_HIM}{\BODY} reports that when the Prophet
\hon{\PBUH}{\BODY} married Fāṭimah \hon{\P_HER}{\BODY} to him, he sent with
them a big sheet, a leather cushion stuffed with barks of the date palm or
idhkhir (scented) grass, a grinder, a leather skin, and two jars. \[Aḥmad\]

ʿAlī ibn Abī Ṭālib \hon{\P_HIM}{\BODY} said, “I married Fāṭimah
\hon{\P_HER}{\BODY}, and we had no bedding except a sheepskin. At night, we
would sleep on it, and during the day we would place fodder on it for our
camel.” \[al-Ṭabaqāt al-Kubrā\]

In another narration, ʿAlī \hon{\P_HIM}{\BODY} states that he told his mother,
Fāṭimah bint Asad, “I will fulfil the outside needs of the house while Fāṭimah
\hon{\P_HER}{\BODY} will serve you in the house by grinding flour, kneading
flour, baking bread, etc.” \[Siyar al-Aʿlām al-Nubalāʾ\]

ʿAlī \hon{\P_HIM}{\BODY} narrated, “Fāṭimah \hon{\P_HER}{\BODY} complained about
the blisters on her hand because of using a millstone. She went to ask the
Prophet \hon{\PBUH}{\BODY} for a servant, but she did not find him (at home) and
had to inform ʿĀʾishah of her need. When he came, ʿĀʾishah informed him about
it.” ʿAlī added, “The Prophet \hon{\PBUH}{\BODY} came to us when we had gone to
our beds. When I was going to get up, he said, 'Stay in your places,' and sat
between us, till I felt the coolness of his feet on my chest. The Prophet
\hon{\PBUH}{\BODY} then said, 'Shall I not tell you of a thing which is better
for you than a servant? When you (both) go to your beds, say "Allāhu Akbar," 34
times, and "Alḥamdulillāh," 33 times, and "Subhan Allah," 33 times. That is
better for you than what you have requested (a servant).'” \[Al-Bukhārī\]

Later, it is reported on the authority of Anas that the Prophet
\hon{\PBUH}{\BODY} brought a slave, whom he had gifted to Fāṭimah. She was
wearing a piece of cloth (which was small) that, if she had to cover her head,
her feet would be exposed, and if she covered her feet, her head would be
exposed. When the Prophet \hon{\PBUH}{\BODY} saw her plight, he consoled her,
saying, "Do not worry. It is only your father and your slave." \[Abū Dāwūd\]

# The Most Noble Mother-In-Law

Hind bint ʿAwf has been given the title of "the Noblest Mother-in-law in
history" by scholars. She was from the tribe of Banū Hilāl. Such were their
marriages that they were called the in-laws of Banū Hāshim. She married thrice
and had six daughters:

Her eldest daughter, Zaynab bint Khuzaymah \hon{\P_HER}{\BODY}, was married to
the Messenger of Allah \hon{\PBUH}{\BODY}.

Her second daughter, Maymūnah bint al-Ḥārith \hon{\P_HER}{\BODY}, was also
married to the Messenger of Allah \hon{\PBUH}{\BODY} after her sister's death.

Her third daughter, Lubābah bint al-Ḥārith (Umm al-Faḍl) or Lubābah al-Kubrá,
was married to ʿAbbās \hon{\P_HIM}{\BODY}, the uncle of the Messenger of Allah
\hon{\PBUH}{\BODY}. She was the first person to embrace Islam after Khadījah
\hon{\P_HER}{\BODY}; some scholars say it was on the same day. She also
breastfed al-Ḥusayn ibn ʿAlī \hon{\P_THM}{\BODY}.

Her fourth (step) daughter, Lubābah al-Ṣughrá, was married to Walīd ibn
al-Mughīrah, which makes her the mother of Sayf Allāh Khālid ibn al-Walīd
\hon{\P_HIM}{\BODY}.

Her fifth daughter, Asmāʾ bint ʿUmays \hon{\P_HER}{\BODY} was married to
Jaʿfar ibn Abī Ṭālib \hon{\P_HIM}{\BODY}. After his death, she married Abū
Bakr al-Ṣiddīq \hon{\P_HIM}{\BODY}. After he passed away, she married ʿAlī
ibn Abī Ṭālib \hon{\P_HIM}{\BODY}.

It is reported that one day, Muḥammad ibn Jaʿfar and Muḥammad ibn Abī Bakr said
to each other in the house of ʿAlī, "My father was superior to yours." ʿAlī
said, "Your mother will decide this matter." Asmāʾ said, "As far as strength is
concerned, Jaʿfar was a very powerful and a great man. As far as religious
practice is concerned, Abū Bakr was a great person." (In other words, both are
great men). ʿAlī then jokingly said to Asmāʾ, "What have you left for me?"
*Subḥan Allāh*. The above conversation was happening in the house of her third
husband about the virtues of the first two husbands to resolve an argument among
the children from the first two husbands.

Her sixth and youngest daughter, Salmá bint ʿUmays, was married to Ḥamzah, the
uncle of the Messenger of Allah \hon{\PBUH}{\BODY}.

She is also the grandmother-in-law of al-Ḥasan and al-Ḥusayn \hon{\P_THM}{\BODY}
via the relationship of her third daughter, Lubābah.

# The Romance of the Prophet \hon{\PBUH}{\BODY}

By looking attentively at the Sīrah of the Prophet \hon{\PBUH}{\BODY}, we find that
he gave us a true, practical, and actual example of what love and romance is.

**1. Knowing Her Feelings**

The Prophet \hon{\PBUH}{\BODY} once said to ʿĀʾishah \hon{\P_HER}{\BODY}, "I
know well when you are pleased or angry with me." ʿĀʾishah [رضي الله
عنها]{dir="rtl"} replied, "How do you know that?" He said, "When you are pleased
with me, you swear by saying, 'By the Lord of Muḥammad,' but when you are angry,
you swear by saying, 'By the Lord of Ibrāhīm.'" She said, "You are right, I
don't mention your name." \[Al-Bukhārī\]

**2. Consoling Her**

Ṣafiyyah \hon{\P_HER}{\BODY} was on a journey with the Prophet
\hon{\PBUH}{\BODY}. She was late, so the Prophet \hon{\PBUH}{\BODY} received her while
she was crying. The Prophet \hon{\PBUH}{\BODY} wiped her tears with his own hands
and tried his utmost to calm her down. \[Al-Sunan al-Kubrá\]

**3. Lying in Her Lap**

The Prophet \hon{\PBUH}{\BODY} would recline in the lap of our beloved mother
ʿĀʾishah \hon{\P_HER}{\BODY} even when she would be menstruating. The
Prophet \hon{\PBUH}{\BODY} would recite the Quran whilst reclining in her lap.
\[Al-Bukhārī\]

**4. Drinking and Eating from the Same Place**

ʿĀʾishah \hon{\P_HER}{\BODY} would drink from a cup. The Prophet
\hon{\PBUH}{\BODY} would take this cup and search for the place where the lips of
his beloved wife made contact. Upon finding the place where his wife drank from
the cup, he would put his lips on the very same place so that his lips would
touch the place where her lips had touched. He would then drink the contents of
the cup at the same time enjoying this union with his spouse. When there was
meat to eat, ʿĀʾishah \hon{\P_HER}{\BODY} would take a bite. The Prophet
\hon{\PBUH}{\BODY} would take the meat from her hand and again place his mouth in
the very same place where his wife had eaten from. This would add a taste of
love to his food. \[Muslim\]

**5. Kissing**

The Prophet \hon{\PBUH}{\BODY} would kiss and embrace his wives while fasting, and
he had more control over his passion than any other person. \[Muslim\]

**6. Lifting A Bite to Her Mouth**

The Prophet \hon{\PBUH}{\BODY} said, "And whatever you spend (for Allah\'s sake) you
will be rewarded for it, even for a morsel of food which you may put in the
mouth of your wife." \[Al-Bukhārī\]

**7. Assisting her in the Housework**

ʿĀʾishah \hon{\P_HER}{\BODY} was asked, "What did the Prophet
\hon{\PBUH}{\BODY} do in his house?" She replied, "He used to keep himself busy
serving his family, and when it was time for prayer, he would go for it."
\[Al-Bukhārī\]

**8. Sharing Happy Occasions with Her**

Once, when the Ethiopians were practicing target shooting in the masjid complex,
the Prophet \hon{\PBUH}{\BODY} stood with his wife watching. Not only did the
Prophet \hon{\PBUH}{\BODY} stand with his wife, but he also put his cloak around
her. The Prophet \hon{\PBUH}{\BODY}, although he had other work to do, stayed there
standing with his wife. He only went when his wife wanted to go. \[Aḥmad\]

**9. Calling Her by a Beautiful Name**

The Prophet \hon{\PBUH}{\BODY} would call his wife ʿĀʾishah [رضي الله
عنها]{dir="rtl"} as "Ḥumayrāʾ" out of love. Linguistically, it means the little
red one, but scholars state that in reality it refers to someone who is so fair
that due to the sun they get a reddish tan. This was the reason why the Prophet
\hon{\PBUH}{\BODY} called her "Ḥumayrāʾ". \[Ibn Mājah\]

He \hon{\PBUH}{\BODY} used to lovingly shorten her name as well. ʿĀʾishah [رضي الله
عنها]{dir="rtl"} said, "Once Allah\'s Messenger \hon{\PBUH}{\BODY} said to me, 'O
ʿĀʾish! This is Jibrīl greeting you.' I said, 'Peace and Allah\'s Mercy and
Blessings be on him, you see what I don\'t see.'" \[Al-Bukhārī\]

Muḥammad ibn Abī Bakr narrates that when ʿĀʾishah \hon{\P_HER}{\BODY}
would become angry, the Messenger of Allah \hon{\PBUH}{\BODY} would stroke her nose
and then say to her, "O ʿUwayyish! Say,

[اَللّٰهُمَّ رَبَّ مُحَمَّدٍ اغْفِرْ لِيْ ذَنْبِيْ ، وَأَذْهِبْ غَيْظَ قَلْبِيْ
، وَأَعِذْنِيْ مِنْ مُضِلَّاتِ الْفِتَن]{dir="rtl"}ِ

'O Allah, Lord of Muḥammad, forgive my sins, remove the anger of my heart, and
protect me from misleading trials.'" \[Ibn al-Sunnī\]

**10. Beautifying Oneself for One's Spouse**

Ibn ʿAbbās [رضي الله عنهما]{dir="rtl"} said, "As my wife adorns herself for me,
I adorn myself for her. I do not want to take all of my rights from her so that
she will not take all of her rights from me, because Allah stated the following,
"And women shall have rights similar to the rights against them." \[Sūrat
al-Baqarah Q2:228\]
